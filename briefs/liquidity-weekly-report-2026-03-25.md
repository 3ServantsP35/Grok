## Executive Summary

**Base case:** Global liquidity remains a headwind through late Q2 2026, transitions toward stabilization in Q3, and becomes a more constructive BTC tailwind in Q4.

For Bitcoin, that implies:
- **Spring:** choppy / fragile / rally-fade risk
- **Summer:** base-building and transition
- **Q4:** best window for sustained upside continuation

**Lead-lag conclusion:** At the cycle level, liquidity leads BTC. At turning points, BTC often front-runs the data by a few weeks.

> **Working rule:** use liquidity as a medium-term leader with an approximate **8-13 week lead** into BTC, while allowing BTC to anticipate turning points by **2-6 weeks** when markets begin discounting easing, QT slowdown, or improving risk appetite.

## Framework

This report uses the CIO macro framework already adopted in the repo:
- **Liquidity leads → Growth follows → Inflation lags → Rates respond**
- BTC is treated primarily as a **liquidity + risk-appetite asset**
- Risk appetite can amplify or dampen the liquidity signal, but does not replace it

### Lead-Lag Assessment

## Structural / cycle level
- Global liquidity remains the primary medium-term driver
- Internal prior CIO work estimates liquidity explains roughly **40-45% of systematic BTC movement**
- Reference lead estimate used here: **~13 weeks**, with a practical working range of **8-13 weeks**

## Inflection-point level
- BTC can bottom or rally **before** the liquidity series looks obviously constructive
- Most likely front-run window: **2-6 weeks**
- Typical catalysts:
  - QT slowdown or perceived QT end
  - Treasury cash drawdown / reduced drain
  - Fed easing expectations
  - improving cross-asset risk tone
  - spillover from external central bank easing

**Conclusion:**
> Liquidity leads the cycle, but BTC can lead the confirmation.
